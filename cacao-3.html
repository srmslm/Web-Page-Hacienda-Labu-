The Origin

Climate, intention,
and expertise.

Hacienda Labu cacao grows under the shade of certified Teak on Nicaragua's North Caribbean Coast — a humid tropical microclimate with 2,400–3,000 mm of annual rainfall and an average temperature of 28°C. These conditions, unique in Central America, produce beans of exceptional flavour complexity across a diverse genetic base.

Our post-harvest team operates a newly inaugurated processing facility developed in close collaboration with internationally recognised cacao consultants. Fermentation and drying protocols are proprietary, refined specifically for our RACCN microclimate — available to qualified buyers under NDA.

The Process

Five steps from harvest to export.

01
Harvest & Pod Break
Selective hand-picking at peak ripeness. Main harvest March–July. Secondary harvest December–February. Pods broken and beans separated from pulp immediately.
02
Fermentation
6–10 days in 500 kg wooden boxes covered with jute sacking. Beans turned every 24 hours to ensure even fermentation. Profile-specific protocols applied — 85–90% fermentation index achieved.
03
Natural Slow-Drying
8–13 days on raised wooden drying beds. Begins at 10 cm layer depth for 3 days, then reduced to 5 cm. Drying time varies with humidity, sunlight, and desired profile.
04
Quality Control
Cut test analysis, moisture measurement, sensory evaluation by trained panel. Very low to no cadmium. Very low to no pesticide residues.
05
Export Preparation
Standard 60 kg jute bags. Moisture ≤7% confirmed at dispatch. Full Rainforest Alliance documentation, phytosanitary certificate, and origin traceability. FOB Corinto — 30–50 day delivery.
